The exercise

Score 12 opportunity cards on Value (1-5) and Effort (1-5) and plot them on the 2x2 (Quick Wins, Strategic, Time Sinks, Fill-ins). Then run mock Weighted Voting in a department-head role. Produce the consolidated segmentation and note what moved.

The 4-quadrant view is the default; the 3-segment variant (Quick Wins, Strategic, Next Bets at high-value/medium-effort) is fine when Fill-Ins and Time Sinks would just create noise.

Reference · The Four Plays (mirror of slide 70)

When Weighted Voting produces a strange result

Tie Break. Two opportunities tie. Break on effort first, sponsor strength second. Pre-declare the rule before the survey.

Domain Weight. One stakeholder dominates. Weight votes by domain (CFO 2x on finance, GC 2x on legal), set with leadership, not in the moment.

Pet Project. A senior sponsor pushes a low-value item. Surface it with its real score, name the trade-off, let leadership decide. Never fight the sponsor in the room.

All 5s / All 1s. A voter rates everything the same. Flatten and force a re-rank: pick your top 5 and rank them.

The plays do not change the scores; they protect the credibility of the scoring.
