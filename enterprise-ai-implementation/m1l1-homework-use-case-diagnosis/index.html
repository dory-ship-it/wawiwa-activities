Homework Step-by-Step Guide

Read instructions from your module requirements and type your answers below.

Important Assignment Scope & Framework Notice:

Expected Length: Exactly 2 pages. Format as a structured document with clean headings.

This diagnosis document is the absolute starting point for the whole module. The same use case will travel with you from lesson to lesson. Once you choose it, do not switch without the instructor's approval.
  • Personal AI use is different from organizational implementation.
  • Most AI failures come from a poor definition of the problem, process, and end user.
  • Before you buy or build, you diagnose.
Section 1 of 9

Use Case Title

A short, clear name that describes the case, 3 to 6 words.
Examples from syllabus: "Sales call summaries", "Handling repeat HR questions", "Triaging customer service requests".
Section 2 of 9

Personal or Organizational?

State the type and explain it in one sentence.

Framework Rule: Ask yourself: who gets hurt if the output is wrong? If it is one person working on their own, that is personal use. If the output feeds a process others depend on, that is organizational implementation.
Section 3 of 9

What is the problem today?

Phrase it precisely with this mandatory structure:
"Today X happens, and it hurts because Y."

Rule: Do not write the solution. Describe the current situation instead. What actually happens? What does it cost the organization? What is not working?
Section 4 of 9

Who is the end user?

The specific role of the person who actually works with the process.

Rule: Not "the team" or "the users." Name them precisely. (e.g., operations manager, service representative, HR coordinator, lawyer. Who exactly?)
Section 5 of 9

What is the current process?

Describe it in 3 to 5 steps: who starts it, what happens in the middle, how it ends.

Guiding questions: Who triggers the process? What information goes in? What happens in the middle? What is the final output? How long does it take?
Section 6 of 9

What repeats?

Which part of the process happens again and again? Which inputs and outputs are similar?

Framework Rule: Repetition matters, because AI helps where there are patterns. What is the same every time? What changes?
Section 7 of 9

Where are the pain points?

Identify wasted time, errors, frustration, delays, or repetitive manual work.

Where do people complain? What takes the most time? Where do errors appear? What does everyone agree is painful?
Section 8 of 9

What is still unclear?

What is missing in order to understand the case better?

What information do you need to check? Who do you need to talk to? Which assumptions are not yet grounded?
Section 9 of 9

Interview: Insights or Questions

If you interviewed someone: 3 to 5 insights. If not: 5 questions you would ask.

Syllabus Rule: A 15 to 30 minute interview with someone who lives the process is worth more than any guess. If you did not get to it, write what you would want to ask.
