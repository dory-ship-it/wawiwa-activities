What you are producing

You take a fictional client through an end-to-end AI Needs Mapping engagement and produce the artifact set a working Implementation Specialist delivers after a 4-week discovery: an AI Opportunity Map, a prioritized shortlist, a phased roadmap, an ROI hypothesis, and a 10-slide executive presentation. You then defend it in a 20-minute mock executive review.

Use this page to plan and track your five artifacts. Each planning box shows a green check when filled. The actual artifacts are built in Excel / Notion / PowerPoint and submitted to your shared folder, this page is your cockpit, not the submission.

Your client & engagement stance

Work within the case pack: 5 interview transcripts, org chart, systems landscape, CEO mandate. Do not invent new departments or stakeholders.

Artifact 1 · AI Opportunity Map 40%

  • 12 to 20 opportunity cards, covering at least 4 of the 5 departments.
  • Each card: ID, Process, Pain (traced to an interview quote), Root Cause (5-Whys / Fishbone), Opportunity, Solution Category (Agent / Automation / BI / Document AI), Tools (April 2026 catalog), Data Sensitivity, Effort, Value, Owner/Sponsor, Pain Owner, Source Interview.
  • At least 3 cards trace to inferred pains (behavior described but not flagged). Process-Redesign cards need an As-Is swimlane; dominant People/Process/Policy/Data branches need a Fishbone.
  • Format: Notion / Airtable / Excel export. Not PDF or image.

Artifact 2 · Prioritized Shortlist 15%

  • Top 5 opportunities with full card detail plus Rationale, Sequencing, Dependencies, and 90-day Success criteria.
  • At least one Quick Win (Phase 1) and one Strategic (Phase 2).
  • Name which of the Four Plays (Tie Break, Domain Weight, Pet Project, All 5s/All 1s) governed any calibration, with the rule pre-declared before scoring.

Artifact 3 · 90-Day Roadmap 15%

  • Phase 1 (next 90 days): the 5 Quick Wins with dates and named owners.
  • Phase 2 (months 4-9): strategic builds with quarterly milestones.
  • Phase 3 (months 10+): foundational tracks (single source of truth, master data, mobility, paperless).
  • Critical-path dependencies + a milestone-quarter view. Format: visual (Mermaid / Lucidchart / Miro / Copilot Gantt). Include the source file.

Artifact 4 · ROI Hypothesis 15%

  • Top 3 opportunities. Show the four-component math end to end: cost baseline (hours × headcount × loaded labor + rework + tooling), expected savings (25-30% demo-to-production buffer), investment (build + license + change management), payback month.
  • Three KPIs each (leading / lagging / guardrail) and a best/base/worst sensitivity.
  • Plus a one-page Qualitative ROI Memo for one opportunity, using one of the eight patterns (Risk Reduction, Employee Experience, Burnout Reduction, Brand Reputation, Learning Velocity, Optionality, Innovation Signaling, Error Reduction).
  • Format: Excel with working formulas (Copilot in Excel), one sheet per opportunity. No pasted values.

Artifact 5 · 10-Slide Executive Presentation 15%

  • Wawiwa Executive Pattern: Cover, Vision, Findings (top 3 patterns), Recommendations (top 5 + one-line ROI), Roadmap, Costs (with break-even), Next steps. Exactly 10 slides, no appendix.
  • Name a real AIBP candidate from the case pack (Sacred Triangle scoring + 20-40% FTE) as the in-house person who carries the work forward. You are the external Specialist; the AIBP is different.
  • Forward references: one line on the Center of Excellence (governance) and one naming the InfoSec interface for your highest-sensitivity opportunity.
  • Prep for the mock review with the "skeptical CFO" AI Q&A simulation.
