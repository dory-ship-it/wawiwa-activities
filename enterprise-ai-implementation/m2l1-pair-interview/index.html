How this works

You will run a 30-minute mock discovery interview in pairs. One of you plays a NorthRiver persona (stay in character); the other plays the Implementation Specialist and runs the interview using the five-category frame. Then the Specialist fills in the Interview Log below. Use the reference cards as needed. The goal is to surface pains, name who owns them, and hear what the persona did not flag.

Reference cards do not need to be filled. The six numbered sections do, each shows a green check when complete.

Reference · The five persona cards

The persona player reads only their own card and stays in character.

A · Sarah Chen — CFO, NorthRiver Holdings

Background: 47, CPA, joined 2019 from real-estate accounting. Tired but committed.

Voice: direct, well-prepared, occasionally exasperated; pushes back on vague questions.

Pains to surface: 14-day month-end close; three accounting environments (Acumen 7, Acumen 9, QuickBooks); audit flagged six vendor-master inconsistencies; Q1 bondholder presentation stress; paper checks to 30% of vendors.

B · Marcus Holloway — Head of Property Operations

Background: 61, institutional memory of the firm.

Voice: starts with "so the way we do it is"; long pauses; tells stories; names properties.

Background: 43, ex-Salesforce admin, most digitally native on the team.

Voice: confident, fast, metric-driven.

D · Robert Pak — General Counsel

E · Lin Zhao — Head of IT

Background: 36, three months into the role.

Voice: direct, frustrated, technical; "fail the audit", "running on luck", "no source of truth".

Pains: no SSO across 8+ systems; manual off-boarding leaks access; no master data layer; three disconnected ERPs; AI policy stuck in legal; 2 outsourced helpdesk staff; a September cyber audit she expects to fail.

Posture: personally pro-AI, professionally worried about uncontrolled usage without policy.

Reference · The five-category question frame

Lead with "describe what you do" before "what hurts". Listen for value the interviewee did not name.

1 · Existing structure and processes

Walk me through your day. What did the team work on this morning? Which three tasks did you do most last week? Show me where the work lives. Then: where does it slow down or get redone?

2 · Challenges and interfaces

When did you last chase another team for something? How often does that repeat? Which interfaces feel heaviest? Where do things get lost across boundaries?

3 · Systems and tools

Open the system you use most, what did you click, copy, paste, or have to remember? What do you wish lived inside the tool? What makes you switch tools mid-task?

4 · Management and control routines

What meetings did you sit through? What reports did you produce or read? How long did the last monthly close take? Which routines are high-leverage vs overhead?

5 · Improvements and opportunities

Tell me about a time something went very well, and a time it went badly. If you had 20% time back on one thing, what would you do with it?

Inferred opportunities the interviewee did not name are the highest-leverage finds, and the most professional risk. Ground each in a quote.

6 · AI synthesis & critical eye

Every AI artifact is a draft. Read it against the source, check for fabricated quotes or invented attributions, and decide whether you would sign your name to it.
